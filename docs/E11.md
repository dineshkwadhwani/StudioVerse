## 1. Purpose

This document defines the business and functional requirements for the **Referrals** capability in StudioVerse.

The purpose of this Epic is to allow Company, Professional, and Individual users to refer new people through a referral link, track those referrals in a separate table, reward the referrer with coins, and support Super Admin-level visibility and reminder actions.

This Epic also defines the menu entry, the referral creation flow, referral status tracking, wallet updates, and reminder mail placeholders.

## 3. Executive Summary

StudioVerse needs a **Referrals** capability so that users can invite new Coaches or Individuals through a referral link and receive coin rewards when those referrals are created and later join the platform.

A new menu item called **References** shall appear in the Manage section. Clicking it shall open the **Manage Referrals** page.

On this page, users shall be able to view referrals they created, filter by referred type using a radio selector, and create new referrals by selecting whether they are referring a Coach or an Individual, then entering the referred person’s email address and phone number.

Each referral shall be tracked in a separate referral table. When a referral is created, a placeholder mail action shall be triggered. The referring user shall receive **10 coins** at the time of referral creation, and an additional **5 coins** when the referred person registers and joins the platform.

If a referred person later registers by themselves and matches an existing referral record, the referral status shall become **Joined**, and the referrer’s wallet shall be updated accordingly.

Super Admin shall also be able to access this functionality, filter referrals by Company, Professional, or Individual, view referral statuses, and initiate reminder mail for selected referrals through a placeholder action.

## 4. Business Context

StudioVerse already uses role-based user models and wallet-based coin logic across other modules.

Referral-based growth introduces an additional acquisition channel where existing users can invite new people into the ecosystem and be rewarded for successful onboarding.

This module is important because it:
- increases organic growth,
- gives users an incentive to refer others,
- creates measurable referral tracking,
- and supports Super Admin monitoring of referral performance.

---

## 5. Problem Statement

StudioVerse currently needs a formal referral system that records who referred whom, how the referral was categorized, whether the referral joined, and how coin rewards should be issued.

Without this capability:
- referral invites are not tracked centrally,
- reward issuance is not auditable,
- joined status is not visible,
- and Super Admin cannot manage referral reminders or view referral performance.

The platform therefore needs a structured referral workflow with tracking, rewards, and admin oversight.

## 16. Reward Logic

### 16.1 Reward on Creation
When a referral is created:
- the referring user shall receive **10 coins**.

### 16.2 Reward on Join
When the referred person later registers and matches the referral record:
- the referrer shall receive **5 additional coins**.

### 16.3 Wallet Update
Coin rewards shall be recorded as wallet transactions.

### 16.4 Reward Timing
The wallet update for the 10-coin reward shall happen at referral creation.  
The wallet update for the 5-coin reward shall happen only after the referred person joins.

---

## 17. Referral Join Logic

### 17.1 Registration Check
When a new person registers by themselves, the system shall check the referral table.

### 17.2 Match Criteria
A referral match shall be based on the referred person’s identity fields, such as email and phone number.

### 17.3 Joined Status
If a matching referral record is found:
- the referral status shall be updated to **Joined**,
- the referrer shall receive the additional 5 coins,
- the wallet transaction shall be updated accordingly.

### 17.4 No Match
If no referral match is found:
- the user shall register normally,
- and no referral reward shall be issued.

## 19. Status Model

### 19.1 Suggested Status Values
The referral status model should support at minimum:
- `Created`
- `Joined`
- `Reminder Sent`

### 19.2 Created
A referral is in Created status when it is first saved.

### 19.3 Joined
A referral is in Joined status when the referred person registers and matches the referral record.

### 19.4 Reminder Sent
A referral may be marked or tracked as Reminder Sent when Super Admin triggers the reminder placeholder action.

## 20. Data Requirements

### 20.1 Referral Entity
The referral table shall support at minimum:

- `id`
- `referrerUserId`
- `referrerRole`
- `referrerCompanyId` (nullable)
- `referredType` (`Coach` or `Individual`)
- `referredEmail`
- `referredPhone`
- `status`
- `createdAt`
- `updatedAt`
- `joinedAt` (nullable)
- `reminderSentAt` (nullable)

### 20.2 Wallet Transaction Linkage
Each referral reward shall create a wallet transaction record with:
- source = referral,
- amount = 10 or 5,
- linked referral record,
- linked user.

### 20.3 Audit Metadata
The system should store enough metadata to trace:
- who created the referral,
- when it was created,
- when the referral joined,
- and when rewards were granted.

## 22. Architectural Recommendation

Referrals should be implemented as a first-class referral domain rather than being embedded inside user creation or wallet logic.

The recommended architecture is:
- one referral table,
- one referral creation service,
- one referral match service during registration,
- one wallet reward service,
- one admin filter and reminder service,
- and placeholder mail hooks for referral and reminder events.

This approach improves:
- traceability,
- reward correctness,
- maintainability,
- and future extensibility.

## 26. Risks and Considerations

- If referral matching is too loose, incorrect users may receive rewards.
- If referral matching is too strict, valid joins may not be recognized.
- If coin issuance is not transactional, wallet balance mismatches may occur.
- If reminder placeholder is not clearly separated, it may be mistaken for production email delivery.
- If multiple referral records exist for the same contact, join logic must define the winner clearly.
