## Implementation status snapshot — April 29, 2026

### Treasury and wallet accounting model — IMPLEMENTED
- Per-tenant treasury wallet at `treasury::<tenantId>` — one wallet per tenant, not tied to any superadmin user.
- Treasury opening balance sourced from tenant config `walletConfig.superAdminOpeningCoins` (default 100,000).
- Registration bonus and referral reward both debit the tenant treasury 1:1 in trusted backend callables.
- Auto top-up logic in callables: if treasury balance is insufficient, it tops up in chunks equal to the configured opening balance and records a ledger entry.
- Idempotent treasury backfill callable for all existing tenants.
- All spend by users returns credits to treasury (platform-owned content path) or to the content creator (creator-owned content path).

### Creator earnings routing — IMPLEMENTED
- `returnDebitsToTreasury` Firestore trigger now acts as the earnings router on every assignment debit:
  - Platform-owned content (`ownerEntityId` blank) → credits return to tenant treasury.
  - Creator-owned content, assigned by non-owner → credits go to creator wallet.
  - Self-assignment (creator assigns their own content) → treasury path; no double-earning.
- Idempotent via `creator-earning-{txId}` marker document.
- Creator earnings ledger entry written with `source: assignment`, `activityType`, `activityId`, `creatorUserId`.

### Company-coach earnings rule — IMPLEMENTED
- `resolveEarningsRecipientForUser` in `functions/src/wallets/treasury.ts` encodes the rule:
  - If a coach has `associatedCompanyId`, all earnings go to the company wallet.
  - If a coach is independent, earnings go to their own wallet.
- Used by creator earnings routing and referral rewards. Must be used by all future earnings flows.

### Standardised transaction source labels — IMPLEMENTED
- All wallet transaction writes use enforced labels: `registration`, `referral`, `assignment`, `promotion`, `manual_offline_allocation`, `cashout`.

# TITLE StudioVerse - EPIC E14 - Revenue Models and Monetization

This Epic defines the revenue engine for StudioVerse. It covers how the platform earns from assessments, platform-owned programs, coach/company-owned programs and events, promotions, lead exposure, and wallet-based settlement flows.

The goal is to support a marketplace where:
- StudioVerse publishes and sells its own content,
- coaches and companies publish their own content,
- users buy credits and spend credits,
- creators earn revenue from eligible transactions,
- and the platform takes fees or commissions where applicable.

---

# TITLE StudioVerse - 1. Purpose

StudioVerse already has a strong base revenue model through assessments and credit purchases. However, if coaches and companies create their own programs and events, the platform needs a second monetization layer so that these transactions also generate revenue.

The goal of this Epic is to support:
- platform-owned content monetization,
- creator-owned public and private content monetization,
- listing fees,
- promotion fees,
- marketplace commissions,
- lead unlock monetization,
- and a payout-ready creator earnings model.

# TITLE StudioVerse - 3. Business Context

StudioVerse already has a strong base revenue model through assessments and credit purchases. However, if coaches and companies create their own programs and events, the platform needs a second monetization layer so that these transactions also generate revenue.

The monetization model should balance three goals:
- keep the UX simple,
- make creators willing to publish content,
- and ensure StudioVerse earns from every meaningful transaction path.

---

# TITLE StudioVerse - 4. Revenue Streams

## 4.1 Platform-Owned Content Revenue

StudioVerse-created assessments, programs, tools, and events remain the highest-conversion direct revenue stream. Users pay credits to access or assign them, and platform revenue is retained fully or according to the existing business rules.

Examples:
- user buys assessment access,
- coach assigns platform-owned program to client,
- company purchases platform-owned event access.

## 4.2 Creator-Owned Content Revenue

Coaches and companies can create their own programs and events. These may be:
- Private: visible only to the creator and their direct users.
- Public: visible across the marketplace.
- Promoted: boosted into featured placement for a time window.

Revenue on creator-owned content comes from:
- listing fee,
- promotion fee,
- marketplace commission,
- lead unlock charges.

## 4.3 Lead Exposure Revenue

StudioVerse can monetize access to lead details and lead visibility. Coaches and companies who want to find prospects can pay to unlock contact details or premium lead access.

## 4.4 Cash-Out Revenue

Creators earn in credits after qualifying sales. Later, they can cash out through a platform-managed payout flow. The platform can retain a spread or cash-out fee once payout support is enabled.

---

# TITLE StudioVerse - 5. Content Visibility Rules

Every coach/company-created program or event must support a visibility mode.

## 5.1 Private
- Visible only to creator and selected direct users.
- Not discoverable in marketplace search.
- No listing fee required if purely private.
- Can still be assigned to the creator’s own clients.

## 5.2 Public
- Visible in marketplace search and browse pages.
- Can be discovered by any eligible user.
- Requires listing fee.
- Eligible for marketplace commission when purchased by non-owner users.

## 5.3 Promoted
- Public content with boosted visibility.
- Shows in home page / landing page / featured area for a limited period.
- Requires promotion fee.
- Promotion duration and placement are managed by Super Admin.

---

# TITLE StudioVerse - 6. Monetization Rules

## 6.1 Listing Fee
A one-time fee applies when a creator publishes a public program or event.

Recommended behavior:
- private content: no listing fee,
- public program: listing fee required,
- public event: listing fee required.

This acts as both a revenue stream and a quality filter.

## 6.2 Promotion Fee
A creator can pay to mark a public program or event as promoted.

Recommended behavior:
- promotion fee is time-based,
- promotion can be per day, week, or campaign window,
- promoted items appear in high-visibility sections.

## 6.3 Marketplace Commission
When a non-owner user buys a public creator-owned program or event, StudioVerse keeps a commission and the creator receives the remainder in creator wallet credits.

Recommended default:
- platform commission: 15%
- creator share: 85%

This can be configurable by Super Admin.

## 6.4 Lead Unlock Fee
If a coach/company wants access to a lead’s contact details or lead profile, a credit fee is charged.

Possible lead actions:
- view lead card free,
- unlock contact details paid,
- unlock advanced lead profile paid.

## 6.5 Creator Cash-Out
Creators accumulate credits in a creator wallet. Later, they can request payout to INR through a future Razorpay-based cash-out flow.

---

# TITLE StudioVerse - 7. Core Revenue Design Decision

A creator should not pay commission when they assign their own private content to their own client. That is not a marketplace sale; it is creator-owned delivery.

Commission should apply when:
- the content is public,
- the buyer is not the creator,
- and the purchase happens through the marketplace or public discovery flow.

This avoids charging creators for selling their own content to their own audience and keeps the model fair.

---

# TITLE StudioVerse - 8. Wallet and Earnings Model

StudioVerse should support two logical balances:
- User wallet for buying and spending credits.
- Creator earnings wallet for earning credits from transactions.

## 8.1 Creator Earnings Wallet
Creator earnings accumulate from:
- marketplace sales,
- possible lead unlock commissions later,
- promotion incentives only if that becomes a business rule.

## 8.2 Cash-Out Readiness
The creator earnings wallet must track:
- earned credits,
- available for cash-out credits,
- pending settlement credits if payout delays are added later.

## 8.3 Cash-Out Phase
Cash-out will be enabled later through Razorpay payouts. For this Epic, the system must be architected so that cash-out can be added without reworking the earnings ledger.

# TITLE StudioVerse - 13. Risks and Mitigations

1. Creators feel overcharged  
Mitigation: no commission on creator self-assignment, public commission only on marketplace sale.

2. Revenue confusion between credits and INR  
Mitigation: separate purchase credits from creator earnings and payout view.

3. Complexity in payout implementation  
Mitigation: defer Razorpay cash-out to later epic, keep current earnings ledger payout-ready.

4. Duplicate wallet credits  
Mitigation: idempotent transaction keys for every monetized event.

5. Low content supply  
Mitigation: use listing fees and promotions to curate quality, while platform-owned content remains always available.
