## 1. Purpose

This document defines the business and functional requirements for the **Cohort Management** capability in StudioVerse.

The purpose of this Epic is to allow **Company** and **Professional** users to create and manage cohorts made up of one Professional and multiple Individuals.

This Epic also defines how cohorts are populated, how new Individuals can be created during cohort setup, how cohort activation depends on Professional assignment, and how activities can be assigned to an entire cohort with wallet coin deduction based on cohort size.

## 3. Executive Summary

StudioVerse needs a **Cohort Management** capability so that Companies and Professionals can organize multiple Individuals under a named cohort and use that cohort as an operational unit.

A cohort shall always contain:
- one Professional,
- and more than one Individual.

When a **Company** creates a cohort, it provides a cohort name and can search existing Individuals by phone number or email to add them to the cohort. If no matching Individual is found, the Company may add a new Individual inline by providing first name, last name, phone number, and email address. These new Individuals are created when the cohort is saved and are then added to that cohort.

A Company may create a cohort without initially assigning a Professional, but the cohort shall become **Active** only when a Professional is assigned. The Company may later edit the cohort and assign a Professional, at which point all Individuals in that cohort also become associated with that Professional.

When a **Professional** creates a cohort, the Professional is automatically assigned to the cohort and automatically associated with all Individuals in that cohort.

When assigning activities, the Company or Professional may assign an activity to the whole cohort. In that case, the activity is assigned to every Individual in the cohort, and the total coins deducted shall equal:

\[
\text{Total Coins Deducted} = \text{Number of Individuals in Cohort} \times \text{Cost of Activity}
\]

The wallet deduction must therefore scale by cohort size.

## 4. Business Context

StudioVerse already supports user roles such as Company, Professional, and Individual, and it also supports assignment of activities such as Programs, Assessments, and Events.

However, operational delivery often happens in groups rather than one person at a time. A cohort provides that group structure and becomes the unit through which a Company or Professional can manage Individuals collectively.

This capability is also important because it reduces repetitive assignment effort, supports group-based delivery models, and aligns naturally with coaching, training, and cohort-driven learning experiences.

---

## 5. Problem Statement

StudioVerse currently needs a formal Cohort Management capability that allows a Company or Professional to create and manage a grouped set of Individuals under a shared cohort identity.

The platform also needs to support both:
- adding existing Individuals to a cohort,
- and creating new Individuals inline when they do not already exist.

Without this capability:
- group-based delivery remains manual,
- activity assignment becomes repetitive and inefficient,
- professional-to-individual linkage may remain fragmented,
- and there is no structured operational unit for cohort-based journeys.

The platform therefore needs a role-aware cohort model with clear composition, activation, editing, and assignment behavior.

## 6. Objectives

The objectives of this Epic are:

- Allow Company to create cohorts.
- Allow Professional to create cohorts.
- Require every cohort to support one Professional and more than one Individual.
- Allow Company to search existing Individuals by phone number or email.
- Allow Company to create a new Individual inline if not found.
- Save newly entered Individuals when the cohort is saved.
- Allow Company to create a cohort with or without immediate Professional assignment.
- Mark a cohort **Active** only when a Professional is assigned.
- Allow Company to edit a cohort later and assign a Professional.
- Associate all cohort Individuals to the Professional once a Professional is assigned.
- Automatically associate the logged-in Professional to the cohort when created by Professional.
- Automatically associate all cohort Individuals to that Professional when created by Professional.
- Allow cohort-level activity assignment.
- Deduct coins based on number of Individuals multiplied by activity cost.
- Keep cohort logic aligned with Manage Users and Activity Assignment.

## 10. Domain Definitions

### 10.1 Cohort
A Cohort is a named group structure that contains one Professional and multiple Individuals.

### 10.2 Active Cohort
An Active Cohort is a cohort that has a Professional assigned and is therefore operationally ready for downstream actions such as guided delivery and assignment.

### 10.3 Draft / Inactive Cohort
A Draft or Inactive Cohort is a cohort that may contain Individuals but does not yet have a Professional assigned.

### 10.4 Cohort Member
A Cohort Member is an Individual who belongs to a cohort.

### 10.5 Cohort Professional
The Cohort Professional is the Professional associated with the cohort.

### 10.6 Assign to Cohort
Assign to Cohort is the action through which an activity is assigned to all Individuals within a cohort.

---

## 11. User Roles

### 11.1 Company
A Company user can:
- create cohorts,
- search and add Individuals,
- create new Individuals inline,
- optionally assign a Professional,
- edit cohorts later,
- and assign activities to cohorts.

### 11.2 Professional
A Professional user can:
- create cohorts,
- add Individuals to cohorts,
- automatically become the Professional for the cohort,
- and assign activities to cohorts.

### 11.3 Individual
An Individual is a member of a cohort but cannot create or manage cohorts.

### 11.4 Super Admin
Super Admin is not the primary actor for cohort creation in this Epic.

---

## 12. Cohort Composition Rules

### 12.1 Required Structure
A cohort shall always conceptually consist of:
- one Professional,
- and more than one Individual.

### 12.2 Professional Requirement for Activation
A cohort created by Company may initially exist without a Professional, but it shall not be marked **Active** until a Professional is assigned.

### 12.3 Individual Minimum
A cohort must contain more than one Individual to meet the expected group definition.

### 12.4 Professional-Created Cohort
When a Professional creates a cohort, that Professional automatically becomes the cohort Professional.

---

## 13. Company Cohort Creation Flow

### 13.1 Create Cohort
When Company creates a cohort, the user shall provide:
- Cohort Name — mandatory

The Company may also:
- search existing Individuals by phone number or email,
- add matched Individuals to the cohort,
- and create new Individuals inline if not found.

### 13.2 Search Existing Individuals
The Company shall search Individuals by:
- phone number,
- or email address.

### 13.3 Found Individual
If a matching Individual is found:
- the system shall display the matching person,
- and allow the Company to add that Individual to the cohort.

### 13.4 Not Found Individual
If no Individual is found:
- the system shall ask whether the user wants to add a new Individual,
- and if yes, the system shall request:
  - first name,
  - last name,
  - phone number,
  - email address.

### 13.5 Save Behavior
When the cohort is saved:
- newly entered Individuals shall be created,
- all selected and newly created Individuals shall be associated to the cohort.

### 13.6 Optional Professional Assignment
During Company cohort creation:
- Professional assignment may be optional,
- the cohort may be saved without a Professional,
- but the cohort shall remain inactive until a Professional is assigned.

---

## 14. Professional Cohort Creation Flow

### 14.1 Create Cohort
When a Professional creates a cohort, the user shall provide:
- Cohort Name — mandatory

The Professional may also:
- add Individuals to the cohort using the supported member-add flow.

### 14.2 Automatic Professional Assignment
When a Professional creates a cohort:
- the logged-in Professional shall automatically become the cohort Professional.

### 14.3 Automatic Individual Association
All Individuals added to a Professional-created cohort shall automatically become associated with that Professional.

### 14.4 Active Status
Because the Professional is already assigned in this creation flow, the cohort can be active once it satisfies the required Individual membership rules.

---

## 15. Edit Cohort Flow

### 15.1 Company Edit Cohort
A Company user shall be able to edit an existing cohort to:
- add or remove Individuals,
- assign or change the Professional,
- and update cohort details such as name.

### 15.2 Assign Professional Later
If a Company-created cohort was saved without a Professional:
- Company may later edit the cohort,
- assign a Professional,
- and upon save, all Individuals in that cohort shall become associated with that Professional.

### 15.3 Status Transition
When a Professional is assigned to a previously inactive cohort:
- the cohort status shall become **Active** if all other validity conditions are satisfied.

---

## 16. Individual Creation During Cohort Setup

### 16.1 Inline User Creation
If an Individual is not found during search, the user may create the Individual inline as part of the cohort creation flow.

### 16.2 Mandatory Fields
The inline new-Individual form shall require:
- First Name
- Last Name
- Phone Number
- Email Address

### 16.3 Deferred Save
The new Individual record shall be created only when the cohort is saved successfully.

### 16.4 Tenant and Association Rules
Any new Individual created during Company cohort setup shall inherit the Company tenant.  
Any new Individual created during Professional cohort setup shall inherit the Professional tenant and become associated with that Professional.

---

## 17. Cohort Status Model

### 17.1 Suggested Status Values
The cohort status model should support at minimum:
- `draft`
- `inactive`
- `active`

### 17.2 Draft / Inactive Meaning
A cohort may remain draft or inactive when:
- it has not yet satisfied minimum structure,
- or it does not yet have a Professional assigned.

### 17.3 Active Meaning
A cohort shall be marked active only when:
- the cohort has a Professional assigned,
- and the cohort meets the minimum expected Individual criteria.

---

## 18. Assign Activity to Cohort

### 18.1 Supported Activity Types
The platform should support assigning the following to a cohort:
- Assessments
- Programs
- Events

### 18.2 Assignment Behavior
When Company or Professional assigns an activity to a cohort:
- the activity shall be assigned to each Individual in the cohort,
- each assignment shall behave like an individual assigned activity,
- and assignment records should remain traceable back to the cohort assignment event.

### 18.3 Coin Deduction Logic
The total coin deduction for cohort assignment shall be:

\[
\text{Total Coins Deducted} = \text{Number of Individuals in Cohort} \times \text{Activity Coin Cost}
\]

### 18.4 Example
If:
- a cohort has 12 Individuals,
- and the assigned Assessment costs 5 coins,

then total coins deducted shall be:

\[
12 \times 5 = 60
\]

### 18.5 Wallet Sufficiency
Cohort assignment shall proceed only if the relevant wallet has enough coins for the full cohort assignment cost.

### 18.6 Insufficient Coins
If sufficient coins are not available:
- cohort assignment shall not proceed,
- no assignments shall be created,
- and no wallet transaction shall be recorded.

### 18.7 Successful Assignment
If sufficient coins are available:
- the system shall confirm the total deduction,
- create assignments for all Individuals in the cohort,
- and create the necessary wallet transaction records.

## 19. User Experience Requirements

### 19.1 Cohort Creation Experience
The cohort creation experience shall be structured and easy to use, allowing users to:
- name the cohort,
- search and add Individuals,
- create missing Individuals inline,
- assign a Professional where applicable,
- and save with clear status feedback.

### 19.2 Search Experience
Search by phone or email shall be quick and clearly indicate:
- whether a match was found,
- whether the result can be added,
- and what to do when no match exists.

### 19.3 Inline Add Experience
When no matching Individual is found, the interface shall clearly ask whether the user wants to add a new Individual.

### 19.4 Status Visibility
The UI shall clearly show whether the cohort is:
- draft,
- inactive,
- or active.

### 19.5 Cohort Assignment Experience
When assigning an activity to a cohort, the UI shall show:
- cohort name,
- number of Individuals,
- activity cost per person,
- total coins required,
- and confirmation before assignment.

### 20.2 Cohort Member Entity
The Cohort Member model shall support at minimum:

- `id`

- `addedAt`

### 20.3 Cohort Assignment Linkage
The system should support linking:
- cohort assignment event,
- source activity,

## 21. Business Rules

- Company can create cohorts.
- Professional can create cohorts.
- A cohort shall support one Professional and more than one Individual.
- Company may create a cohort without immediate Professional assignment.
- A Company-created cohort shall become active only when a Professional is assigned.
- Company can search Individuals by phone or email during cohort creation.
- If no Individual is found, the user may add a new Individual inline.
- New Individuals are created when the cohort is saved.
- All selected or newly created Individuals are assigned to the cohort on save.
- When Company later assigns a Professional to a cohort, all cohort Individuals become associated to that Professional.
- When Professional creates a cohort, the Professional is automatically assigned to the cohort.
- When Professional creates a cohort, all cohort Individuals are automatically associated to that Professional.
- Company and Professional can assign activities to the whole cohort.
- Total cohort assignment coins deducted equal member count multiplied by activity cost.
- Cohort assignment must fail fully if wallet balance is insufficient for the full amount.

## 22. Architectural Recommendation

Cohort Management should be implemented as a reusable cohort domain service rather than embedding cohort logic separately inside Programs, Events, or user lists.

The recommended architecture is:
- one canonical Cohort entity,
- one Cohort Member relationship model,
- one role-aware cohort creation service,
- one shared individual lookup and inline-create flow,
- one cohort activation rules engine,
- and one cohort assignment orchestration service.

This approach improves:
- consistency,
- maintainability,
- reuse,
- auditability,
- and future extensibility.

## 26. Risks and Considerations

- If member count rules are not enforced properly, cohorts may not match the intended group model.
- If inline-created Individuals are saved incorrectly, duplicates may increase.
- If later Professional assignment does not backfill Individual association correctly, downstream journeys may break.
- If cohort assignment is not transaction-safe, some members may receive activities while others do not.
- If wallet deduction is not calculated from final member count correctly, billing and entitlement errors may occur.
